Introduction

The entire globe has evolved into a global community. We have joined the global community as citizens. What happens in one area of the world, no matter far away, impacts us. According to a song, “it’s a tiny world, after all.” And it is hoped that we are able to propose solutions to these global problems.

So, to honor this ground, ponder by the question below:

As an educator, how would you devise a hypothetical solution to the problems we face today?

A Second Life

Refer to the following premise below:

Say you happened to die suddenly in a terrible accident in a world plagued by problems like climate change, pollution, violence, terrorism, and corruption. You wished to change something, even a tiny difference. But it all ended too soon.

Assuming reincarnation is real, which of the United Nations' 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) would you wish to see solved in your next life, and why?

The Top Ten Global Issues

(derived from Turner, 2018)

  1. Climate Change

    Global temperatures are rising, and by 2100, they are expected to rise from 2.6 degrees Celsius to 4.8 degrees Celsius. This would result in more severe weather, food, and resource shortages, and disease transmission.

    To put it simply, global warming causes climate change.

  2. Pollution

    Ocean trash, pesticides, and fertilizers, as well as air, light, and noise pollution, are all examples of such. Clean water is vital for humans and animals, but more than a billion do not have access to it because of harmful chemicals, unmaintained sewages, industrial wastes, etc.

  3. Violence

    Whether it is a city-wide war, hatred directed at a specific group of people, or sexual harassment on the street, violence is prevalent but an avoidable problem that has gone unaddressed for far too long.

    Type Description
    Physical Violence occurs when someone uses a part of their body or an object to control a person’s actions
    Psychological Violence occurs when someone uses threats and causes fear in an individual to gain control
    Sexual Violence occurs when a person is forced to unwillingly take part in a sexual activity
    Spiritual Violence occurs when someone uses an individual’s spiritual beliefs to manipulate, dominate, or control that person
    Emotional Violence occurs when someone says or does something to make a person feel naive or worthless.
    Cultural Violence occurs when an individual is harmed as a result of practices that are part of their culture, religion, or tradition

    (Violence Prevention Initiative, 2014)

    In terms of “bullying” in the country, we have the RA 10627, or the Anti-Bullying Act of 2013, to address these issues in schools.

  4. Security and Well-Being

    This involves the safety of an individual in their homes, the proximity of healthcare to people, etc. The United Nations began a momentum on abolishing issues involving these. International treaties, agreements, and pacts—to name a few.

    As Muston (2022) stated, “students who do not feel safe at school—either emotionally or physically—are more distracted and less successful than their peers. School safety and student achievement are connected; it’s hard to have one without the other.”

  5. Lack of Education

    More than 72 million children of primary education age around the world remain unschooled due to marginalization, poverty, financial deficit, and inequality (Humanium.org, 2018).

  6. Unemployment

    Many people, particularly those aged 15 to 24, struggle to obtain work and provide a decent living for themselves and their families. This results in a scarcity of essential resources such as food, clothes, transportation, and adequate living circumstances.

  7. Government Corruption

    Graft, bribery, embezzlement, backdoor deals, nepotism, and favoritism are all examples of corruption. As a major source of poverty, corruption disproportionately affects the poor, undermining political and economic growth, democracy, and other aspects of life. As of Transparency International’s 2013 corruption ranking, the Philippines stood at 94th among 177 nations (Moss, 2014).

  8. Malnourishment and Hunger

    According to World Health Organization (2021),

    “Malnutrition, in all its forms, includes undernutrition (wasting, stunting, being underweight), insufficient vitamins and minerals, overweight, obesity, and the resultant diet-related non-communicable illnesses.”

    It is a serious and lasting burden that impacts the developmental, economic, social, and medical sectors of many individuals and families, communities, and countries.

  9. Substance Abuse

    It is the “harmful or hazardous use of psychoactive substances, including alcohol and illicit drugs” (WHO, 2021).

    As the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (2022) indicated, “around 284 million people aged 15-64 used drugs worldwide in 2020, a 26 percent increase over the previous decade.” And in the Philippines, the prevalence of drug use is 2.05%, equivalent to 1.7 million Filipinos (UN Philippines, 2021.)

  10. Terrorism

    As the OHCHR (n.d.) implied, terrorism “involves the intimidation or coercion of populations or governments through the threat or perpetration of violence, causing death, serious injury or the taking of hostages.” It diminishes the public's sense of security and threatens democracy. Schools are one of the hotspots of these attacks.

    The study by Wansink et. al. (2021) revealed that teachers should first initiate a safe, calm classroom environment while monitoring students’ reactions. Teachers should also provide accurate information and help them make sense of what is happening in a neutral, coherent narration. And finally, and most importantly, listen and abide by the instructions of authorities.

The 17 Sustainable Development Goals

In September 2015, The General Assembly adopted the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development which includes 17 SDGs. Building on the principle of “leaving no one behind,” the new Agenda emphasizes a holistic approach to achieving sustainable development (UN News, 2015).

The UN Secretary-General, Ban Ki-moon, said:

“The seventeen Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are our shared vision of humanity and a social contract between the world's leaders and the people… They are a to-do list for people and the planet, and a blueprint for success,”

No. Type Description
1 No Poverty End poverty in all its form everywhere
2 Zero Hunger End hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture
3 Good Health and Well-being Ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages
4 Quality Education Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote life-long learning opportunities for all
5 Gender Equality Achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls
6 Clean Water and Sanitation Ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all
7 Affordable and Clean Energy Ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable, and modern energy for all
8 Decent Work and Economic Growth Promote sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment and decent work for all
9 Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure Build resilient infrastructure, promote inclusive and sustainable industrialization and foster innovation
10 Reduced Inequalities Reduce inequality within and among countries
11 Sustainable Cities and Communities Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, and resilient and sustainable
12 Responsible Consumption and Production Ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns
13 Climate Action Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts (in line with the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change)
14 Life below Water Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas and marine resources for sustainable development
15 Life on Land Protect, restore, and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, sustainably manage forests, combat desertification, and halt and reverse land degradation and half biodiversity loss
16 Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions Promote peaceful and inclusive societies for sustainable development, provide access to justice for all and build effective, accountable and inclusive insitututions at all levels
17 Partnerships for the Goals Strengthen the means of implementation and revitalize the global partnership for sustainable development
