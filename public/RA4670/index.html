Introduction

The Magna Carta for Public School Teachers, also known as Republic Act No. 4670, outlines the professional rights and protections that our public school teachers are entitled to in light of the demands and degree of difficulty involved in their profession. It was approved in 1966 and composed of 7 chapters and 35 sections.

Magna Carta, which means 'The Great Charter,’ is legal writing that establishes the rights and privileges of an entity.

The term came from Europe when King John of England signed a charter of liberties as a countermeasure to the threat of civil war.

I. Declaration of Policy Coverage

Section 1: Declaration of Policy

The act’s policy is to promote and improve

  1. the social and economic status of public school teachers,
  2. their living and working conditions,
  3. their terms of employment, and
  4. their career prospects so they may compare favorably with other career opportunities; while
  5. attracting more on the teaching profession with proper qualifications.

Section 2: Title Definition

This act shall be known as the “Magna Carta for Public School Teachers” and shall apply to public school teachers except those in the professional staff of state colleges and universities.

The term “teacher” shall mean all personnel engaged in classroom teaching, at any level, on a full-time basis (including guidance counselors, school librarians, industrial arts or vocational instructors, supervisors, administrators, etc.) but shall not include school nurses, school physicians, school dentists, and other school employees.

II. Recruitment and Career

Section 3. Recruitment and Qualification

With respect to DepEd’s existing recruitment policy, the following shall constitute the minimum education qualifications for teacher applicants:

Sector Minimum Requirement(s)
Kindergartens and Elementary Grades
  • Bachelor’s degree in Elementary Education
Secondary
  • Bachelor’s degree in Education or its equivalent
  • Bachelor’s degree in Arts or Science with at least 18 professional units in Education
Secondary Vocational (and 2 Year Technical Courses)
  • Bachelor’s degree in the field of specialization with at least 18 professional units in Education
Collegiate Levels
  • Master’s Degree with the specific area of specialization

In certain cases, the school superintendent may appoint–on temporary status–a teacher during the absence of eligible applicants.

Moreover, all applicants are mandated to take competitive examinations in which preference in hiring is affected by their respective ranks in the examination.

Section 4. Probationary Period

Hired teachers who possess the minimum educational qualifications, but lack the appropriate civil service eligibility, are put into provisional status for a year or so.

Section 5. Tenure of Office

Stability on employment and security of tenure shall be assured the teachers as provided under existing laws.

Moreover, a teacher with provisional status shall be held in an extended permanent status if the teacher rendered at least 10 years of continuous, efficient, and faithful service in the position.

Section 6. Consent for Transfer Transportation Expenses

“No teacher shall be transferred without his consent from one station to another.”

The teacher subject to an unjustified transfer can appeal his/her case to the Director of Public Schools or the Director of Vocational Education. If the pending case is approved, the transfer is rejected.

Transfers in the three months before any local or national election are not allowed.

The Government covers the necessary transfer expenses of the teacher and his/her family if the transfer is approved.

Section 7. Code of Professional Conduct for Teachers

Each teacher must have an available copy of the Code of Professional Conduct for Teachers. Alternatively, the office of the school principal or head teacher must hold copies of the Code where they may be accessible to teachers.

Section 8. Safeguards in Disciplinary Procedure

Teachers subject to any disciplinary procedure shall have

  • the right to be informed, in writing, of the charges;
  • the right to full access to the evidence in the case;
  • the right to defend oneself and to be defended by a representative of his choice and/or by his organization
  • the right to appeal to designated authorities

Moreover, no publicity shall be imposed against a teacher during the pendency of the case.

Section 9. Administrative Charges

Formal administrative charges against a teacher shall go first on the committee of the School Superintendent of the Division or duly authorized eligible parties in the division where the teacher belongs. The responsible authorities will formulate recommendations to the Director of Public Schools.

Section 10. No Discrimination

“There shall be no discrimination whatsoever in the entrance to the teaching profession, or during its exercise, or in the termination of services, based on other than professional consideration.”

Section 11. Married Teachers

“Whenever possible, the proper authorities shall take all steps to enable married couples, both of whom are public school teachers, to be employed in the same locality.”

Section 12. Academic Freedom

“Teachers shall enjoy academic freedom in the discharge of their professional duties, particularly with regard to teaching and classroom methods.”

Academic freedom is "the freedom of teachers and students to teach, study, and pursue knowledge and research without unreasonable interference or restriction" (Britannica, 2020).

III. Hours of Work and Renumeration

Section 13. Teaching Hours

Actual classroom instruction shall not be required to be more than six hours a day for the teacher to have time for preparation, correction of student’s works, and other work incidentals to the teaching duty.

Exigencies of service may require the teacher to render more than six hours a day, but not exceeding eight hours, of classroom instruction.

Section 14. Additional Compensation

Additional compensation is given to teachers engaging in co-curricular and out-of-school activities and any other activities outside of the normal duties of any teacher. The additional compensation is equivalent to at least 25% of his/her regular remuneration.

Co-curricular activities are inclined to the school’s academic curriculum while extra-curricular ones are not; that is, hosting a math club is co-curricular and coaching a volleyball team is extra-curricular.

In the case of those teachers and school officials not engaged in actual classroom instruction, they gain compensation of 25% of their regular remuneration on the excess of 8 work hours.

In alignment with this section, other agencies utilizing the services of teachers shall pay the additional compensation required.

Section 15. Criteria for Salaries

Teacher salaries shall correspond to the following criteria:

  • they shall compare favorably with those paid in other occupations requiring equivalent or similar qualifications, training, and abilities;
  • they shall be such as to insure teachers a reasonable standard of life for themselves and their families; and
  • they shall be properly graded so as to recognize the fact that certain positions require higher qualifications and greater responsibility than others.

Section 16. Salary Scale

If the teacher’s efficiency rating is at least satisfactory, the teacher’s salary will follow a gradual progression by means of regular increments, granted automatically after three years.

Section 17. Equality in Salary Scales

Teachers’ salary scales managed by the municipal or provincial government shall not be less than the provided by the National Government.

Section 18. Cost of Living Allowance

“Teacher's salaries shall, at the very least, keep pace with the rise in the cost of living by the payment of a cost-of-living allowance.”

Annually, the Secretary of Education shall recommend to Congress the appropriation of the necessary funds for the cost-of-living allowances of teachers employed by the National Government.

Section 19. Special Hardship Allowances

Teachers subject to hardship such as difficulty commuting to the place of work or other hazards present to the place of employment shall be compensated special hardship allowances equivalent to at least 25% of their monthly salary.

Section 20. Salaries to be Paid in Legal Tender

Salaries of teachers shall be paid corresponding to the Philippine currency or its equivalent in checks or treasury warrants.

Section 21. Deductions Prohibited

Salaries of teachers are not deductible whatsoever except

  • lawful dues and fees owing to the Philippine Public School Teacher Association, and
  • premiums property due on insurance policies.

IV. Health Measures and Injury Benefits

Section 22. Medical Examination and Treatment

Teachers are mandated to have a free-of-charge medical examination before they take up teaching, repeating annually. Medical examinations requiring medical treatment and/or hospitalization are provided free by the government entity responsible.

Teachers may also take necessary medical care with the right to be repaid for their traveling expenses if the facility is reasonably distant.

Section 23. Compensation for Injuries

Teachers shall be protected against employment injuries (including physical and nervous strain) and shall be recognized as compensable in accordance with existing laws.

V. Leave and Retirement Benefits

Section 24. Study Leave

Teachers shall be entitled to study leave in accordance with the school calendar not exceeding one school year after seven years of service.

During the leave, teachers can get at least 60% of their monthly salary for a year. This can extend if and only if the teacher needs an additional semester to finish his thesis for a graduate study in education or allied courses.

The teacher must take the regular study load and pass at least 70% of his courses in order to gain his/her compensation.

Section 25. Indefinite Leave

“An indefinite sick leave of absence shall be granted to teachers when the nature of the illness demands a long treatment that will exceed one year at the least.”

Section 26. Salary Increase Upon Retirement

Teachers of eligible retirement age who fulfilled the minimum service years shall be given one range salary raise upon retirement, which becomes the basis for the sum of the retirement pay and the monthly benefits thereafter.

VI. Teacher's Organization

Section 27. Freedom to Organize

Teachers has the right to establish and join organizations of their choosing, local or national, to defend their interests.

Section 28. Discrimination Against Teachers Prohibited

It shall be unlawful for any person to commit any acts of discrimination or dismissal against teachers because of his membership or participation in an organization.

VII. Administration and Enforcement

Section 32. Penal Provision

A person who interferes with the exercise of the rights guaranteed by this Act, upon conviction, be punished by a fine of ₱100 - ₱1000, or by imprisonment, at the discretion of the court.

If the offender is a public official, the court shall order his dismissal from the Government service.
