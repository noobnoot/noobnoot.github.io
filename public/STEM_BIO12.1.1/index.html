What is Biology?

Biology is the scientific study of life and living organisms. It covers everything from microscopic bacteria to complex plants and animals, including humans. Through biology, we explore one of the universe’s mysteries: life.

In this lesson, you are expected to:

  1. Explain biology and its branches.
  2. Examine the usage of basic science process skills and the scientific method.
  3. Describe theories on the origin of life.

There are three major branches of biological science: (1) zoology (animals), (2) botany (plants), and (3) microbiology (microorganisms).

Studying Biology

Biology, like other sciences, uses a systematic process called scientific inquiry—the process by which scientists explore the natural world and propose explanations based on evidence. Under scientific inquiry:

  1. Scientific Process Skills—basic skills used during investigation.

    SkillsExample
    ObservingNoticing differences in plant leaf color
    InferringGuessing the plants lack sunlight
    MeasuringMeasures light exposure time (in minutes)
    ClassifyingGrouping plants based on sunlight exposure (e.g., full sun, partial shade, or low light)
    CommunicatingLeaf color changed faster on longer sun-exposed plants
    PredictingSun-exposed plants will survive longer

  2. Scientific Method—a systematic step-by-step approach to solving scientific problems.

    StepsExample
    Make an observationYou noticed that your classmates who drink coffee can recite more words.
    Ask a questionDoes caffeine affect a person’s memory?
    Form the hypothesisCaffeine can make a person recite 1 or more words.
    Make a predictionIf they drink a cup of coffee before class, they will recite more words than those who do not.
    Do an experiment Divide participants into two groups. Make them memorize 10 words.
    1. Caffeine-stimulated classmates (experimental group)
    2. Non-stimulated classmates (control group)
    Analyze the result Compare the results from the groups:
    1. Caffeine-stimulated participants recalled an average of 9 words.
    2. Non-stimulated classmates averaged 7 words.
    Report the resultThe result supports the hypothesis. Caffeine does affect memory.

Do note that these examples are oversimplified. Actual usage of these may vary based on the scientific approach.

Theories on Life's Origin

Going back 4.5 billion years, the Earth had no oxygen-rich atmosphere. Extreme volcanic activity was common, and the oceans were filled with simple chemical compounds. Lightning, heat, and extreme UV radiation created a volatile environment that set the stage for the emergence of life.

By this, refer to the question below.

How did life first begin and flourish in such a primitive world?

Origin of Life

Over time, scientists and philosophers have proposed several ideas to explain how life might have started:

  1. Creationism claims that a divine or supernatural being created all life forms.
  2. Abiogenesis claims that the origin of the first living organisms came from nonliving matter. It proposed that simple organic molecules gradually combined to create the first primitive single-celled organism (referred to as the universal common ancestor).

    Warning: Spontaneous generation is NOT abiogenesis. Spontaneous generation is an old, disproven belief that life continuously and suddenly appears from non-living materials. Meanwhile, abiogenesis claims that the first life originated from non-living matter under the specific conditions of early Earth, billions of years ago.

  3. Panspermia suggests that life may have originated elsewhere in the universe and was brought to Earth via meteorites or comets.

Continuation of Life

Once the first life forms emerged, Earth’s conditions continued to change. Scientists have developed several theories to explain how life continued, survived, and transformed.

  1. Biogenesis states that living organisms can only arise from other organisms, not from non-living matter.

  2. Catastrophism suggests that sudden, violent events shaped Earth’s life. Mass extinctions gave way for new life to evolve and adapt.
  3. Evolution explains how living organisms changed via natural selection and genetic variation. It explains diversity and adaptation of species.
