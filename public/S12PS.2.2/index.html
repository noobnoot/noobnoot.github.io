Introduction

Assume you are driving on a highway at a speed of 50 km/h. How many hours will it take you to reach a destination 120 km away?

To solve questions like this, we resort to the branch of physics that deals with motion—kinematics.

Kinematics is the study of motion. It focuses on measurable quantities such as distance, speed, and acceleration.

To know the answer to the problem, we have to understand the concept of distance, time, and speed.

Distance and Speed

When describing motion with kinematics, you must know how far an object is.

Distance ($d$) is the total length of the path traveled by an object. Meanwhile, displacement ($\vec{d}$) tells you the change of distance (with direction considered).

Dividing distance and displacement by time $t$ gives you speed and velocity, respectively.

Speed ($v$) is the rate at which an object covers distance. Similarly, velocity ($\vec{v}$) is the rate of change of displacement over time with direction. They are defined as:

$v = \frac{d}{t}$ (for distance) or $\vec{v} = \frac{\vec{d}}{t}$ (for displacement)

So, how do we solve the motorcycle problem?

The problem defines distance $d=120 \text{ km}$ and $v=50 \text{ km/h}$, solving for time $t$. Using the speed formula, we can solve for time $t$:

$$v = \frac{d}{t} \to 50 \text{ km/h} = \frac{120 \text{ km}}{t} \to t = \frac{120 \text{ km}}{50 \text{ km/h}}=\boxed{2.4 \text{ h}}$$

So, you still have 2.4 hours to reach the destination.

An object moving on an $x$-axis (like a motorcycle coursing through a straight road) is considered horizontal motion.

Other examples of horizontal motion include a ball rolling on the floor, a car speeding up on a highway, or you jogging towards school.
